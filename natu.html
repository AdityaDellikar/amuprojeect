INTRODUCTION: Earth provides us with natural resources, such as air, water, and food, which are essential for our survival and welfare.
Introduction to natural resources: Natural resources are substances or materials from nature that humans use to sustain life, and have economic, ecological, scientific, aesthetic, and cultural values.Natural resources include oil, coal, natural gas, metals, stone, and sand. Other natural resources from nature are air, sunlight, soil, and water.

YPES OF NATURAL RESOURCES ARE
1. Renewable Resources or Exhaustible Resources: Renewable resources are substances available in large amounts in nature. These resources are infinite and can be repeatedly used—for example, water, air, sunlight, etc.
2. Non-Renewable Resources or Inexhaustible Resources: These resources are also available in nature but are limited. They may end after continuous usage—for example, oil and natural gas, minerals, coal, etc.
EXAMPLES OF NATURAL RESOURCES: Oil, coal, natural gas, metals, stone and sand are natural resources. Other natural resources are air, sunlight, soil and water. Animals, birds, fish and plants are natural resources as well. Natural resources are used to make food, fuel and raw materials for the production of goods.
